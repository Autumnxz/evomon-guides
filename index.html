01 — Order of operations

Do these four things, in this order

1

Redeem codes before you spend anything

Codes are entered from the settings cog (top-right) → scroll down → enter code → Ok. Do this before buying anything with coins so you don't waste currency you were about to get for free.

2

Pick one starter and commit

Bubble, Blazpup, or Leafbun. Whichever you choose, feed it all your early EXP Fruit — don't split resources across your whole box yet.

3

Clear Verdant Valley → Petal Pond → first Island Boss

This is the intended early route. Match your starter's type against what's dominant on each island rather than over-leveling through brute force.

4

Treat Level 30 as a wall, not a milestone

Level 30 unlocks your Ultimate Ability and the Ascension questline. Don't rush past it undergeared — finish Ascension immediately once you hit it.

05 — Fast answers

Before you commit longer

What is Evomon?

A Roblox monster-catching game — catch, train, and battle over 200 Evomons across 9 types, with island-based progression toward a Level 30 power spike and late-game systems like Ascension and Rifts.

Which starter should I actually pick?

Bubble if you want the smoothest early game — it matches the type weaknesses of the first boss fights. Pick Blazpup only if you're comfortable playing around a weaker early matchup for a faster long-term clear speed.

Can I switch starters later?

Not meaningfully — you can catch other Evomon, but your starter is meant to be your first fully-invested team member. Don't spread early EXP Fruit across multiple catches.

What should I prioritize before Level 30?

Player EXP over creature EXP — it gates Ascension and Evolutions. Daily quests are the single best Player EXP source early on.
